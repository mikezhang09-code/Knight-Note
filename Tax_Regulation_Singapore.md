# Singapore Tax and Investment Regulations Summary

## Personal Income Tax in Singapore
- Singapore uses a progressive tax rate for personal income tax, ranging from 0% to 24%.
- Income up to SGD 20,000 is exempt from tax.
- Tax residents are taxed on income such as wages, rental income, self-employed income, interest, and dividends.
- Non-residents typically pay a flat rate of 24% without personal reliefs.
- Tax filing is annual, for income earned in the previous calendar year.
- Example tax rates for residents:
  - SGD 0–20,000: 0%
  - SGD 20,001–30,000: 2%
  - SGD 30,001–40,000: 3.5%
  - SGD 40,001–80,000: 7%
  - Up to SGD 1,000,000+: 24%

## Capital Gains Tax
- Singapore does not levy capital gains tax on investors, including non-citizens.
- Gains from sale of assets like stocks or property are generally not taxed unless the activity is deemed trading income.

## Non-Citizen Investment in Singapore Stock Market
- Foreigners can invest in Singapore stocks by opening a Central Depository (CDP) account and a trading account with a licensed broker.
- Required documents include a valid passport, proof of residential address, and a Singapore bank account.
- Foreign ownership restrictions exist only in certain strategic sectors.
- No capital gains tax applies to foreign investors.

## Corporate Tax for Investment Companies
- Investment companies incorporated in Singapore are subject to a corporate tax rate of 17% on taxable income.
- Companies must file tax returns annually and submit Estimated Chargeable Income (ECI) reports.
- Tax exemptions and rebates may apply, including for startups.
- Singapore applies OECD Pillar 2 global minimum tax rules for large multinational enterprises from 2025.
- Gains from sale of shares are generally not taxable unless related to trading.
- Special tax incentives exist for fund management and certain investment activities.
